along my travels, i've met kind and beautiful people, and captured scenic moments not to showcase photography skill but simply to collect inspiration which is A PURE AND SIMPLE SOURCE OF ENERGY for the idea and my Creative work.

LAOS

A quiet and peaceful piece of the world has been become a part of me. ​Those moss-covered ancient temple roofs, the distant chants echoing through the misty morning air, the gentle waves of the Mekong, the smoky scents from wood-fired kitchens, the early markets, the smell of local dishes, those rainy evenings, the gentle smile of local people, the sound of bamboo rustling and the bell from temples... These small, humble and familiar things can heal broken souls significantly. It could awaken a forgotten energy and  love that have always root inside us. Laotian and Laos is one of the countries i love the most so far ❤️
​

MYANMAR

Watching sunset drape over the ancient stupas and temples of Old Bagan was an unforgettable experience. I tried to capture that beautiful scene as much as i could and hope someday these memories will find their way into my art.

TAIWAN

​What I love the most are the roofs of traditional temples and pagodas. They’re such great references for drawing Chinese or even Wuxia-style buildings, both in terms of exterior and interior. ​The carvings on the pillars and reliefs are also incredibly beautiful and detailed..

INDONESIA

Those statues and stone fountains covered in roots and moss are mesmerizing. They’ve greatly influenced my style when designing similar elements. I’m also drawn to the gates and arches with their unique patterns as they inspired me creating a piece called ''Step Well"
​

PHILIPPINES

​What impressed me the most were the shades of green, turquoise, and grey found in the trees and plants, crystal-clear water, and rocks or mountains. There are many islands here. The sea creatures and corals also inspired me a lot when I worked on the decoration of the aquarium world in a pixel art project Let's buil a zoo: Aquarium Odyssey

​

CAMBODIA

Those ancient temples feel almost otherworldly. Those statues, the intricate carvings on stone walls, and the overall architecture are simply breathtaking. They became a deep source of inspiration for me when I created the pixel temple called "Panduranga" and "Amavarati"


INDIA

The hustle and bustle of the streets can be overwhelming at times with the spicy smells, the mix of sounds, the explosion of colors everywhere, all of that is what makes India so unique. I was mesmerized  by the architecture and the intricate patterns of the forts, tombs and castles. Still, what left the strongest impression on me were the narrow alleys and old streets, with crumbling walls and weathered houses. That’s what inspired me to create a piece called "Slumdog".
​The city by the Ganges River, Varanasi, seems to never sleep, always alive with ceremonies and pilgrims. One sad thing I saw was many elderly homeless people living along the Ganges, waiting for death. For them, dying by the river and having their ashes scattered into it means reaching Nirvana and escaping the cycle of suffering.​
 i hope will have a chance to put these inspiration into use in a project one day
​

​SINGAPORE

Even though there are many of skyscrapers, I really love how they’ve planted so many trees. Amid all the concrete and hot glass of the office towers, there are always patches of greenery and cool ponds tucked in between.
I also like the interesting blend of modern architecture with old neighborhoods that still have traditional Chinese rooftops and lanterns.
It feels like a great reference for a game or asset that mixes modern and classic architectural styles.
​

SRI LANKA


I truly fell in love with this country and found so much peace, inspiration and new ideas here! Sri Lanka has everything an artist could ever dream of: mountains, oceans, lush forests, green tea hills, mysterious caves, ancient temples, ancient painting on rock/cave walls and breathtaking ruins like the sacred city in Polonnaruwa. But what amazed me the most was the Lion Rock in Sigiriya, it's absolutely mind blowing to think that ancient people were able to build such a massive structure on top of a towering rock without using any modern path to get up there. It's beyond imagination. ​It’s all so refreshing for the soul and fulfill inspiration . I truly hope for a chance turning some of these ideas into a real project one day
​

JAPAN

It must spend years to travel all sort of places in Japan. I love everything even all little details. From cities to urbans i could feel same feeling deep sense of peace.

THAILAND

I haven't visited many places in Thailand even though i've been here twice and transited countless times here. Most of the time i stayed in the monastery, and when the retreat ended, i went straight to the airport as i would like to keep the energy of mindfulness. Perhaps i should set some time to truly explore Thailand in the near furture.

MALAYSIA

​I don’t quite know how to express just how much I miss Malaysia. Maybe it’s a sense of familiarity like someone returning his country after being away for a while. I miss the taste of dishes: Nasi Lemak, Hainanese Chicken Rice, Ipoh Chicken Hor Fun, Teh O Ice... I prefer the calm and reserved nature of the people here. I like those weekend markets, the leafy neighborhoods, the quiet streets, the sunny day of trekking trails, the sudden rains that come and go, the Mamak restoran and food courts where you could meet all kind of local people. But above all, what I miss the most when I’m away from Malaysia are those roads seen through the window of train or the peaceful houses of familiar neighborhoods through shuttle bus while riding to the train station. Maybe it’s because of the little everyday things which ones we don’t really notice until one day, when they’re no longer there, we realize they had quietly become part of our soul. In fact, some places feel a little old, like a quiet and aging people though it's exactly why i love it so deeply.
​

NEPAL

This was a dream trekking journey. I’m not a mountain enthusiast though for a long time, there has been a deep calling within me, a longing to witness the majestic, sacred snow covered peaks of the Himalayas. For various reasons I postponed it several times, but over time, that calling only grew stronger, and eventually I decided to pick up my backpack and go. It was incredible as everything felt like a dream coming true. I met wonderful trekking companions who were incredibly supportive. The higher we climbed, the thinner the air and the quicker the fatigue, but what struck me most was the overwhelming sense of peace and happiness that filled my soul. It was a powerful source of energy perfect for refreshing the mind and rekindling creativity. Beyond the breathtaking mountains, the winding trails, the stone walled thatched houses, the vegetable gardens, haystacks, those Mules carrying goods up to mountains, their bells echoing softly in the air, the suspension bridges, the sacred temples, the festivals, coountless deities and stories behind them… everything offered an endless stream of inspiration. 
​Looking back, I’m not even sure if it’s the breathtaking views I miss more, or the lovely friends I met along the trail, short encounters though they left me with so many warm memories. This experience inspired me to make " Tea house " 
​

KOREA

I really enjoyed Korea, it’s amazing how the country blends modern skyscrapers with traditional beauty. Right in the heart of the city, you can find ancient palaces with stunning rooftops that feel more like art than architecture. I also loved exploring the culture village with their unique charm and classic designs. It really inspired me. I hope to one day work on a project that mixes the sci-fi or cyberpunk themes with this kind of traditional Eastern architecture. The mix of old and new, East and West, feels like something truly special.
